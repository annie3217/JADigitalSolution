I. Sample Templates

1. The "Easy-Yes" Project Proposal

Best for: VAs and freelancers who want to look professional.

[Your Name/Logo]
Project Proposal for: [Client Name]

The Goal: To streamline [Client's Pain Point] so you can reclaim [X] hours per week to focus on [Client's Goal].

Scope of Services:
[Task 1] | [Task 2] | [Task 3]

Investment: Monthly Retainer: $[Amount] per month.

Next Steps: Review agreement, click "Accept," and receive your invoice.

2. The "Freelancer-Protector" Contract

1. Services: Scope as outlined in the Proposal. Extra tasks billed at $[Rate]/hour.
2. Payment: Due 15 days from the 1st of each month. 5% late fee applies.
3. Termination: 14 days’ written notice.
4. Confidentiality: All business info and passwords kept strictly confidential.
5. Ownership: Deliverables belong to the Client upon full payment.

Signatures: ____________________ Date: ________

II. How to Share These Templates

  • Canva: Copy text blocks into a "Proposal" template. Add brand colors/headshot for a high-converting PDF.
  • Google Docs: File > Share > "Anyone with the link can view."
  • Notion: Create a "Client Portal" page; paste these sections into toggle-lists.

III. "Fill-in-the-Blank" Versions

Proposal: Focus on [Pain Point], [Benefit], and [Number] hours reclaimed.

Contract: Clearly state payment dates, termination notice ([Number] days), and confidentiality.

Welcome Email: Include the Google Drive link and the next check-in date/time.

Pro-Tip: Always use a professional header and export as PDF. Use the "One-Link" strategy (Calendly + Payment) in your signature.

🚀 The Freelancer’s Pre-Send Confidence Checklist

  • [ ] Human Connection: Did I use their name and acknowledge their specific problem?
  • [ ] Professionalism: Did I attach/link the contract? Is pricing clear?
  • [ ] Mindset: Am I asking for a partnership, not begging for a favor? Am I prepared for any answer?

"You don’t need to be perfect to be a professional. You just need to be clear, honest, and reliable."

Managing "Proposal Anxiety"

  • Wait 10 Minutes: Step away after writing to spot typos and gain a fresh perspective.
  • Visualize the "Yes": Spend 10 seconds imagining the client feeling relieved.
  • Focus on the "Why": Every proposal is a step toward independence.
